UAT vs UPT Token Comparison Guide

Version: 1.0 Date: March 18, 2026 INVESTOR EDUCATION

Quick Answer

Token Type Description Best For
UAT (Asset Token) Single asset investment (like buying one stock) Sophisticated investors seeking specific asset exposure
UPT (Pool Token) Diversified pool investment (like an ETF) Investors seeking diversified, lower-risk exposure

1. Defining the Tokens

UAT — Ujamaa Asset Token

Ownership in a single, specific asset. Your return depends entirely on the performance of that one asset.

"Like buying shares in Apple only."

Examples:
  • Lagos Commercial REIT
  • Ivory Coast Cocoa Bond
  • Ghana Gold Revenue
UPT — Ujamaa Pool Token

Ownership in a diversified pool of many assets. Your return is averaged across the entire pool.

"Like buying an ETF or Index Fund."

Examples:
  • Industrial Pool (50+ factories)
  • Harvest Pool (100+ farms)
  • Trade Finance Pool (200+ invoices)

2. Key Differences at a Glance

Feature UAT (Single Asset) UPT (Diversified Pool)
What You Own Share in ONE asset Share in MANY assets
Diversification None Yes (High)
Risk Level Higher (Concentrated) Lower (Diversified)
Expected Return 8-15% (Variable) 8-12% APR (Stable)
Minimum Investment €50,000+ (Typical) €1,000 (Standard)

4. Which Should You Choose?

Choose UAT If:

  • You are a sophisticated or accredited investor.
  • You have deep knowledge of a specific asset class or region.
  • You want potentially higher returns and accept higher risk.
  • Typical for: Family offices, sector experts, HNWIs.

Choose UPT If:

  • You want diversified exposure across many African RWAs.
  • You prefer stable, predictable returns (8-12% APR).
  • You want easier due diligence (pool-level vs asset-level).
  • Typical for: Pension funds, retail investors, first-time RWA investors.

5. The Barbell Approach

Many investors choose to own both to balance their portfolio:

Example Portfolio:
  • 70% UPT (Core): Diversified stability across 150+ assets.
  • 30% UAT (Satellite): High-conviction bets on specific properties or harvests.

Result: A diversified core with targeted high-convexity opportunities.
