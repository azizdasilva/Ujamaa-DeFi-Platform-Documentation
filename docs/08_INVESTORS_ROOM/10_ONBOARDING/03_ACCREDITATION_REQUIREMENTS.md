# Important Notice

**Accredited investor status allows access to certain investment offerings that are not available to retail investors.** Accreditation must be verified according to the laws of your jurisdiction.

**Misrepresentation of accredited investor status is a violation of securities laws** and may result in:
- Immediate account suspension
- Forced redemption of tokens at NAV (which may be below purchase price)
- Legal action for securities fraud
- Reporting to relevant regulatory authorities

# 1. What is an Accredited Investor?

## 1.1 Definition

An **accredited investor** is an individual or entity that meets specific income, net worth, or professional qualifications that allow them to invest in securities that are not registered with financial regulatory authorities.

**Rationale:** Accredited investors are presumed to:
- Have sufficient financial sophistication to understand investment risks
- Have the ability to bear economic losses
- Have access to information typically provided in registered offerings
- Not require the full protections of retail investor regulations

## 1.2 Benefits of Accredited Status

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Access to More Offerings** | Many private placements are limited to accredited investors only |
| **Higher Investment Limits** | No cap on investment amounts (retail investors may have limits) |
| **Reduced Disclosure Requirements** | Some offerings have simplified disclosure for accredited investors |
| **Earlier Access** | Accredited-only rounds may open before retail rounds |

## 1.3 Responsibilities

| Responsibility | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Accurate Representation** | Must truthfully represent financial status |
| **Timely Updates** | Must notify platform of material changes in financial status |
| **Annual Re-certification** | Must re-verify accreditation status annually |
| **Tax Compliance** | Responsible for reporting investment income to tax authorities |

# 2. Accreditation by Jurisdiction

## 2.1 United States (SEC Reg D)

### Individual Accreditation

**Income Test:**
| Requirement | Individual | Joint with Spouse |
|-------------|------------|-------------------|
| **Annual Income** | >$200,000 | >$300,000 |
| **Duration** | Each of last 2 years | Each of last 2 years |
| **Expectation** | Reasonable expectation of same in current year |

**Net Worth Test:**
| Requirement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| **Individual or Joint Net Worth** | >$1,000,000 |
| **Exclusions** | Primary residence excluded |
| **Joint with Spouse** | Can include spouse's assets even if spouse is not accredited |

**Professional Credentials (No Financial Test Required):**
- SEC-registered investment adviser
- Licensed broker (Series 7, 65, 82)
- Licensed attorney in good standing
- Certified Public Accountant (CPA)
- Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA)
- Private fund general partner or director

**Knowledgeable Employees:**
- Directors, executive officers, or general partners of the issuer
- Employees participating in investment activities (for private funds)

### Entity Accreditation

| Entity Type | Requirements |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Corporation/Partnership** | Total assets >$5M; not formed for purpose of acquiring securities |
| **Trust** | Total assets >$5M; not formed for purpose of acquiring securities; purchase directed by sophisticated person |
| **Entity (all equity owners accredited)** | All equity owners must be accredited investors |
| **Investment Company** | Registered under Investment Company Act of 1940 |
| **Business Development Company** | As defined in Section 202(a)(22) of Investment Advisers Act |
| **Family Office** | >$5M in assets under management; not formed for specific investment |
| **Family Client** | Owned exclusively by family clients |

## 2.2 European Union (MiFID II Professional Client)

### Per Se Professional Clients (Automatic)

| Entity Type | Classification |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Credit Institutions** | Banks, authorized under CRD IV |
| **Investment Firms** | Authorized under MiFID II |
| **Insurance Companies** | Authorized under Solvency II |
| **UCITS & AIFs** | Collective investment schemes and managers |
| **Pension Funds** | Regulated pension schemes |
| **Commodity Dealers** | Authorized under MiFID II |
| **Sovereign Wealth Funds** | National and regional governments, central banks |
| **Supranational Organizations** | World Bank, IMF, ECB, EIB, etc. |
| **Large Corporations** | Meeting 2 of 3: (1) €20M balance sheet, (2) €40M turnover, (3) €2M equity |

### Elective Professional Clients (Upon Request)

Individuals or entities can request professional client status if they meet **2 of 3 criteria**:

| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Portfolio Size** | Financial instrument portfolio >€500,000 |
| **Trading Activity** | 10+ transactions per quarter (average) over last 4 quarters |
| **Professional Experience** | 1+ year in financial sector requiring knowledge of investments |

**Procedure:**
1. Submit written request to platform
2. Platform assesses criteria
3. Investor receives written warning of protections lost
4. Investor acknowledges in writing
5. Status granted (can be revoked by investor at any time)

## 2.3 Nigeria (SEC Rules on Digital Tokens)

### High Net Worth Individual

| Requirement | Threshold |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Net Worth** | >₦100,000,000 (excluding primary residence) |
| **OR Annual Income** | >₦25,000,000 for last 2 years |
| **Documentation** | Audited financial statements or bank certification |

### High Net Worth Entity

| Requirement | Threshold |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Net Worth** | >₦500,000,000 |
| **OR Annual Turnover** | >₦250,000,000 |
| **Documentation** | Audited financial statements |

### Sophisticated Investor

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Financial Institution** | Bank, insurance company, fund manager licensed in Nigeria |
| **Pension Fund** | Licensed pension fund administrator |
| **Government Entity** | Federal/state government agency |
| **High Net Worth Individual** | As defined above |

## 2.4 Kenya (Capital Markets Authority)

### Accredited Investor

| Requirement | Threshold |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Annual Income** | >KES 10,000,000 for last 2 years |
| **OR Net Worth** | >KES 50,000,000 (excluding primary residence) |
| **Documentation** | Tax returns, bank statements, or auditor certification |

### Institutional Investor

| Entity Type | Requirements |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Licensed Entity** | Licensed by CMA, CBK, or IRA |
| **Government Entity** | National or county government |
| **International Institution** | World Bank, IFC, AfDB, etc. |
| **Collective Scheme** | Authorized unit trust or mutual fund |

## 2.5 South Africa (FSCA)

### Qualified Investor (FAIS Act)

| Category | Requirements |
|----------|--------------|
| **Natural Person** | Annual income >ZAR 5M OR net worth >ZAR 20M |
| **Company/Trust** | Total assets >ZAR 25M |
| **Institutional** | Licensed financial institution, pension fund, or government entity |

### FSP License Holders

Financial Services Providers licensed by FSCA are automatically qualified investors.

## 2.6 Mauritius (FSC)

### Sophisticated Investor

| Requirement | Threshold |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Investment Amount** | Minimum initial investment of USD 100,000 |
| **OR Net Worth** | Net worth >USD 1,000,000 |
| **Documentation** | Bank reference letter or audited financials |

# 3. Verification Process

## 3.1 Verification Methods (US Reg D 506(c))

For US offerings under Reg D 506(c), issuers must take **reasonable steps to verify** accredited investor status.

### Method 1: Income Verification

| Document | Requirements |
|----------|--------------|
| **IRS Form W-2** | Last 2 years |
| **Tax Returns** | IRS Form 1040 for last 2 years |
| **Employment Verification** | Letter from employer confirming salary and reasonable expectation of continuation |
| **Pay Stubs** | Last 3 months (supplementary) |

### Method 2: Net Worth Verification

| Document | Requirements |
|----------|--------------|
| **Bank/Brokerage Statements** | Last 3 months; must show account holder name |
| **Real Estate Appraisal** | Licensed appraisal for primary residence (to exclude) |
| **Credit Report** | From one of three major bureaus (to assess liabilities) |
| **Investment Account Statements** | Last 3 months |

### Method 3: Professional Credential Verification

| Credential | Verification Method |
|------------|---------------------|
| **Series 7/65/82 License** | FINRA BrokerCheck |
| **Attorney License** | State bar association verification |
| **CPA License** | State board of accountancy verification |
| **CFA Charter** | CFA Institute verification |

### Method 4: Third-Party Verification Letter

A written confirmation from a qualified third party:

| Third Party | Requirements |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Registered Investment Adviser** | SEC or state registered; RIA relationship documented |
| **Licensed Attorney** | Active bar membership; legal opinion letter |
| **Certified Public Accountant** | Active CPA license; signed letter on letterhead |
| **Broker-Dealer** | FINRA member; written confirmation |

**Letter Requirements:**
- On professional letterhead
- Dated within last 3 months
- States specific basis for verification
- Includes third party's credentials and license number
- Signed by authorized representative

# 4. Required Documentation

## 4.1 Individual Investors — Income Test

| Document | Specification | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **IRS Form W-2** | Last 2 years; all pages | Shows wages, tips, compensation |
| **Tax Return (Form 1040)** | Last 2 years; all schedules | May redact SSN (show last 4 only) |
| **Employment Letter** | On company letterhead; signed by HR | Confirms current salary and expected continuation |
| **Pay Stubs** | Last 3 months; supplementary | Shows year-to-date earnings |

**Calculation Example:**
```
Year 1 Income: $220,000
Year 2 Income: $240,000
Current Year Expected: $250,000

✓ Meets $200,000 threshold for individual
✓ Consistent income pattern
✓ Reasonable expectation of continuation
```

## 4.2 Individual Investors — Net Worth Test

| Document | Specification | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| **Bank Statements** | Last 3 months; all accounts | Checking, savings, CDs |
| **Brokerage Statements** | Last 3 months | Stocks, bonds, mutual funds |
| **Retirement Account Statements** | Last 3 months | 401(k), IRA (may have early withdrawal penalties) |
| **Real Estate Appraisal** | Licensed appraisal; <12 months | For investment properties |
| **Primary Residence Appraisal** | Licensed appraisal; <12 months | To determine value to EXCLUDE |
| **Credit Report** | From Equifax, Experian, or TransUnion | To assess total liabilities |
| **Loan Statements** | Last statement | Mortgages, personal loans, credit cards |

**Net Worth Calculation:**
```
ASSETS:
  Cash & Equivalents          $500,000
  Investment Accounts         $800,000
  Retirement Accounts         $400,000
  Investment Real Estate      $600,000
  Personal Property           $200,000
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Total Assets               $2,500,000

LIABILITIES:
  Mortgage (investment)       $300,000
  Mortgage (primary)*         $400,000
  Credit Cards                 $20,000
  Personal Loans               $30,000
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Total Liabilities           $750,000

*Note: Primary residence mortgage excluded if loan > home value

NET WORTH CALCULATION:
  Total Assets               $2,500,000
  Less: Primary Residence    ($500,000) [appraised value]
  Less: Total Liabilities    ($750,000)
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Net Worth                  $1,250,000 ✓

✓ Exceeds $1,000,000 threshold
```

## 4.4 Entity Investors

| Document | Description | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|

# 5. Institutional Investors

## 5.1 Automatic Accreditation (Per Se Professional)

The following entities are automatically accredited without financial thresholds:

| Entity Type | Documentation Required |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Bank** | Banking license; regulatory registration |
| **Broker-Dealer** | FINRA/SEC registration |
| **Insurance Company** | Insurance license; NAIC number |
| **Investment Company** | SEC registration; CIK number |
| **Registered Investment Adviser** | SEC or state RIA registration; Form ADV |
| **Pension Fund** | Plan documentation; ERISA status |
| **Government Entity** | Government authorization |
| **Family Office** | Formation documents; AUM verification |

## 5.2 Family Office Accreditation

**Requirements under SEC rules:**

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Assets Under Management** | >$5,000,000 |
| **Not Formed for Specific Investment** | Established for bona fide family wealth management |
| **Family Clients Only** | Manages assets only for family clients |
| **Family Definition** | Lineal descendants (up to 10 generations), spouses, former spouses |

**Documentation:**
- Family office formation documents
- List of family clients
- AUM certification
- Family tree (for client verification)

## 5.3 Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs)

**When investors pool capital via SPV:**

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **SPV Accreditation** | SPV itself must be accredited (>$5M assets) |
| **OR All Equity Owners Accredited** | Every investor in SPV must be accredited |
| **SPV Manager** | Must be sophisticated/experienced |
| **Not Formed for Specific Investment** | Exception: SPVs for specific offerings are permitted |

# 6. Ongoing Requirements

## 6.1 Re-certification

| Investor Type | Frequency | Process |
|---------------|-----------|---------|
| **Individual** | Annually | Self-certification + random audit |
| **Entity** | Annually | Updated financial statements |
| **Elective Professional (EU)** | Every 2 years | Re-assessment of criteria |

**Re-certification Process:**
1. Email notification 30 days before expiry
2. Complete updated accreditation questionnaire
3. Upload new documents if thresholds changed
4. Re-certify representations

## 6.2 Material Change Reporting

Investors must notify platform within 30 days of:

| Change | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Decrease in Income** | Job loss, reduced compensation |
| **Decrease in Net Worth** | Significant investment losses, major liability |
| **Change in Employment** | Leaving financial services role |
| **Change in Entity Status** | Dissolution, merger, asset sale |
| **Change in UBO** | Ownership structure change |

## 6.3 Loss of Accreditation

**If accreditation is lost:**

| Consequence | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **No New Investments** | Cannot subscribe to accredited-only offerings |
| **Existing Holdings** | Unaffected; can continue to hold |
| **Transfer Rights** | Can transfer to other accredited investors only |
| **Redemption Rights** | Per offering terms (may be restricted) |

**Reinstatement:**
- Must re-qualify under accreditation criteria
- Complete full verification process
- No guaranteed reinstatement
