📖 Glossary of Terms

Competitive Intelligence — Key Terms with Ujamaa-Specific Context

📅 Document Info

Version: 1.0 | Date: March 31, 2026 | Purpose: Define competitive intelligence terms with Ujamaa-specific context and comparisons

ℹ️ How to Use This Glossary

This glossary focuses on terms used in competitive analysis, not general DeFi terminology. Each entry includes:

  • Standard Definition: Industry-wide meaning
  • Ujamaa Context: How it applies to Ujamaa DeFi Platform specifically
  • Competitive Relevance: Why it matters for competitor comparison

🔤A

AUM (Assets Under Management)

Standard Definition: The total market value of assets that a platform or fund manages on behalf of clients.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Target: €50M Year 1, scaling to €1.5B by Year 5.

Revenue Impact: AUM directly drives management fee revenue (2% annually).

Competitive Relevance: Used to compare platform scale. Centrifuge ($1.3B) and Ondo ($2B) have significantly larger AUM than Ujamaa's target, indicating first-mover advantage.

AfCFTA (African Continental Free Trade Area)

Standard Definition: A continental trade bloc aiming to create a single market for goods and services across 54 African countries.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Strategic Alignment: Ujamaa's Pan-African mission aligns with AfCFTA objectives. Platform designed to facilitate cross-border SME financing across OHADA and broader African markets.

Competitive Relevance: Most Class 1/2 competitors lack AfCFTA alignment. This is a differentiator for Ujamaa in African market positioning.

🔤B

Bank Escrow

Standard Definition: A financial arrangement where a third-party bank holds and regulates payment of funds between transacting parties.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Partners: BIIC (Banque Internationale pour l'Industrie et le Commerce) and MCB Mauritius.

Function: Holds investor funds before deployment to SMEs, providing institutional-grade security and regulatory compliance.

Competitive Relevance: Most DeFi competitors (Centrifuge, Goldfinch) operate without traditional bank escrow. This is a key institutional differentiator for Ujamaa.

BRVM (Bourse Régionale des Valeurs Mobilières)

Standard Definition: Regional Stock Exchange serving 8 West African countries (OHADA zone), headquartered in Abidjan, Côte d'Ivoire.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Future Listing: Target for secondary market trading of uLP tokens (18-24 month roadmap).

Strategic Value: Would be first tokenized security listed on BRVM, providing liquidity for investors.

Competitive Relevance: No Class 1 competitor has BRVM listing. First-mover advantage in African traditional finance integration.

🔤C

Class 1 / Class 2 / Class 3 Competitors

Standard Definition: Competitive classification based on threat level and business similarity.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Classification Method: Based on Weighted Similarity Score (WSS) using 8 criteria:

  • Class 1 (WSS 65-100): Direct competitors — Centrifuge, Goldfinch, Ondo Finance
  • Class 2 (WSS 35-64): Indirect competitors — Maple, Securitize, TrueFi, Tokeny (11 total)
  • Class 3 (WSS 0-34): Peripheral competitors — Aave, Compound, African fintechs (14 total)

Competitive Relevance: Determines monitoring cadence: Class 1 = monthly reviews, Class 2/3 = quarterly reviews.

CFG Token

Standard Definition: Native governance token of Centrifuge protocol.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Comparison: CFG is governance-only, not yield-bearing. Ujamaa's uLP token is yield-bearing ERC-3643 security token.

Differentiator: uLP provides direct yield from SME loan interest; CFG provides governance rights only.

Competitive Relevance: Token design difference reflects different target users (DeFi natives vs. institutions).

Competitive Intelligence (CI)

Standard Definition: Systematic collection and analysis of information about competitors to support strategic decision-making.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Scope: 28 competitors tracked across 3 classes.

Owner: Competitive Intelligence Lead

Output: Monthly Class 1 reports, quarterly Class 2/3 reviews, ad-hoc alert escalations.

Competitive Relevance: Enables proactive response to competitive moves (e.g., Africa expansion announcements).

🔤D

DFI (Development Finance Institution)

Standard Definition: Government-backed or multilateral investment institutions that provide capital for economic development in emerging markets.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Target Users: DFIs are key institutional investors for uLP tokens (e.g., AfDB, BOAD, DEG).

Strategic Fit: DFI mandate aligns with Ujamaa's UN SDG goals (SDG 1, 8, 9, 10).

Competitive Relevance: Goldfinch and Centrifuge also target DFIs, but Ujamaa's Africa-first focus provides stronger alignment.

🔤E

ERC-3643 (T-REX Protocol)

Standard Definition: Token standard for permissioned, compliant securities on Ethereum. Includes identity verification and transfer restrictions.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Implementation: uLP tokens and Guarantee Tokens are ERC-3643 compliant.

Features: KYC/AML enforcement, investor accreditation checks, sanctions screening, transfer restrictions.

Competitive Relevance: Key differentiator vs. Class 1 competitors (Centrifuge uses custom NFT standard, Goldfinch uses ERC-20). Only Tokeny and Securitize also use ERC-3643.

EUROD (Ondo Euro Fund)

Standard Definition: Euro-denominated tokenized treasury fund by Ondo Finance, providing yield in EUR rather than USD.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Integration: Ujamaa uses EUROD for yield denomination to match European institutional investor preferences.

FX Risk: Eliminates EUR/USD currency risk for European LPs investing in African SMEs.

Competitive Relevance: Ondo is both a competitor (Class 1) and a technology partner (EUROD integration). Dual relationship.

🔤G

GDIZ (Glo-Djigbé Industrial Zone)

Standard Definition: Large-scale industrial zone in Benin, hosting 10+ factories for textile, agribusiness, and light manufacturing.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Partnership: Primary SME origination partner. GDIZ certifies factories for financing eligibility.

Exclusivity: Ujamaa negotiating exclusive origination agreement with GDIZ.

Competitive Relevance: No Class 1 competitor has GDIZ partnership. Local industrial network access is a moat.

Guarantee Token

Standard Definition: Non-transferable ERC-3643 NFT representing certified merchandise or collateral backing a loan.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Function: SMEs deposit goods (cotton, cocoa, cashews) as collateral; Guarantee Token represents ownership and quality certification.

Innovation: Enables collateralized lending for SMEs lacking traditional collateral (real estate, cash).

Competitive Relevance: Unique to Ujamaa. Goldfinch uses uncollateralized lending; Centrifuge uses invoice/receivable collateral. Guarantee Token is novel for commodity-based collateral.

🔤L

Liquidity Pool

Standard Definition: A smart contract holding locked capital that enables decentralized lending, borrowing, or trading.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Pool Types: Pool Industrie, Pool Agriculture, Pool Trade Finance, Pool Renewable Energy.

Yield Source: Interest from SME loans (12% APR target), minus credit losses (5% provision) and fees.

Competitive Relevance: Centrifuge (Tinlake pools) and Goldfinch (credit pools) use similar models. Ujamaa differentiates via African SME focus and collateral structure.

🔤M

Mauritius FSC (Financial Services Commission)

Standard Definition: Regulatory body for financial services in Mauritius. Issues CIS (Collective Investment Scheme) licenses.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

License Target: Category 1 CIS Manager license (Q2 2026 target).

Regulatory Benefits: Enables legal fundraising from institutional investors, compliance with FATF standards.

Competitive Relevance: No Class 1 competitor holds Mauritius FSC license. First-mover regulatory advantage in African offshore hub.

MCDA (Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis)

Standard Definition: A structured framework for evaluating alternatives across multiple, often conflicting criteria.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Application: Foundation for Weighted Similarity Score (WSS) methodology.

Criteria: 8 dimensions (Geography, User Segment, Product Overlap, etc.) with assigned weights.

Competitive Relevance: Provides objective, replicable scoring vs. subjective competitor ranking.

🔤O

OHADA

Standard Definition: Organisation for the Harmonisation of Corporate Law in Africa. 17 member states with unified business law framework.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Primary Market: OHADA zone is Ujamaa's initial target (West/Central Africa).

Legal Advantage: Single legal framework across 17 countries simplifies cross-border SME financing.

Competitive Relevance: No Class 1 competitor has OHADA-specific legal expertise. Centrifuge, Goldfinch, Ondo are US/EU-focused.

ONCHAINID

Standard Definition: Decentralized identity protocol for KYC/AML compliance. Integrated with ERC-3643.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Integration: Used for investor identity verification and accreditation checks.

Compliance: Ensures only verified, accredited investors can hold uLP tokens.

Competitive Relevance: Tokeny also uses ONCHAINID. Centrifuge and Goldfinch use different compliance solutions.

🔤R

RWA (Real-World Assets)

Standard Definition: Physical or traditional financial assets (real estate, commodities, bonds, invoices) tokenized on blockchain.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Asset Types: SME loans, agricultural commodities, trade receivables, renewable energy projects.

Tokenization: ERC-3643 tokens representing fractional ownership in diversified pools.

Competitive Relevance: All Class 1 competitors are RWA protocols. Ujamaa differentiates via African SME focus vs. US private credit (Centrifuge) or treasuries (Ondo).

🔤S

SME Financing Gap

Standard Definition: The difference between SME financing needs and available credit. World Bank estimates $5.2T global gap, $331B in Africa.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Target: Addressing African portion of gap (€50M Year 1, scaling to €1.5B Year 5).

Impact: 200+ SMEs financed, 5,000+ jobs created (5-year target).

Competitive Relevance: Goldfinch also targets SME lending in Africa (Nigeria, Kenya). Ujamaa's collateral model (Guarantee Token) vs. Goldfinch's uncollateralized approach.

SIPI (Société d'Investissement et de Promotion Industrielle)

Standard Definition: Investment promotion agency in Côte d'Ivoire, supporting industrial development.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Partnership Status: In discussion for SME origination and co-investment.

Competitive Relevance: Local investment promotion agencies are gatekeepers to SME deal flow. No Class 1 competitor has SIPI relationship.

🔤T

TVL (Total Value Locked)

Standard Definition: Total dollar value of assets deposited in a DeFi protocol at a given time.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Target: €50M Year 1 (significantly smaller than established players).

Growth Path: €150M Year 2, €400M Year 3, €800M Year 4, €1.5B Year 5.

Competitive Relevance: Combined competitor TVL ~$100B. Ujamaa's €50M target is <0.1% of incumbent scale, indicating significant growth opportunity and first-mover disadvantage.

Tinlake

Standard Definition: Centrifuge's dApp for creating and managing asset pools. Enables investors to provide liquidity to specific RWA pools.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Comparison: Ujamaa's liquidity pool interface is similar but focused on African SMEs vs. Centrifuge's global private credit.

Competitive Relevance: Tinlake is proven ($1.3B+ tokenized). Ujamaa must demonstrate similar reliability with African assets.

🔤U

uLP Token (Ujamaa Liquidity Provider Token)

Standard Definition: Yield-bearing ERC-3643 token representing ownership in a Ujamaa liquidity pool.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Tokenomics: Value accrual model (NAV increases, balance constant), 180-365 day lock-up, €100K minimum.

Yield: 10-15% APR target (EUROD-denominated), quarterly distribution.

Compliance: ERC-3643 transfer restrictions, KYC/AML enforced, accredited investors only.

Competitive Relevance: Different from Centrifuge's DROP/TIN (tranche tokens) and Goldfinch's uncollateralized pool shares. uLP is yield-bearing security token with institutional compliance.

Ubuntu Philosophy

Standard Definition: African philosophical concept meaning "I am because we are" — emphasizing community, interconnectedness, and collective prosperity.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Mission Alignment: Ujamaa (Swahili for "familyhood") is named after this philosophy.

Impact Goals: Pan-African economic development, 5,000+ jobs, UN SDG alignment.

Competitive Relevance: No Class 1 competitor has explicit African philosophical foundation. Differentiates Ujamaa's mission-driven approach vs. pure-profit competitors.

🔤W

WSS (Weighted Similarity Score)

Standard Definition: Quantitative score (0-100) measuring business similarity between Ujamaa and a competitor using 8 weighted criteria.

🟦 Ujamaa Context

Formula: WSS = Σ(Criterion Score × Weight) × 20

Criteria: Geography (20%), Product Overlap (20%), User Segment (15%), Tech Stack (10%), Business Model (10%), Social Mandate (10%), Token Economy (8%), Partnerships (7%).

Classification: Class 1 (65-100), Class 2 (35-64), Class 3 (0-34).

Competitive Relevance: Objective scoring enables defensible competitor prioritization. See Classification Framework and Worked Example: Centrifuge for full methodology.
