## 2. CREDIT & DEFAULT RISK

### 2.1 Industrial Default Risk

**Risk Description:**

Industrial borrowers may fail to repay their financings due to:
- Business failure or bankruLPcy
- Cash flow problems
- Off-taker non-payment (e.g., ZARA doesn't pay invoice)
- Production issues (equipment failure, supply chain disruLPion)
- Force majeure (natural disasters, pandemics, political unrest)

**Expected Impact:**

| Scenario | Default Rate | Investor Impact |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Base Case** | 5% | -3% NAV (covered by provision) |
| **Adverse Case** | 15% | -12% NAV (reserve drawdown) |
| **Severe Adverse** | 25% | -25% NAV (significant loss) |
| **Black Swan** | 40% | -45% NAV (recapitalization needed) |

**Mitigation Measures:**

| Mitigation | Effectiveness | Residual Risk |
|------------|---------------|---------------|
| **GDIZ Guarantee** | High (GDIZ backs repayment) | GDIZ credit risk |
| **5% Credit Provision** | Medium (covers expected losses) | Insufficient for stress |
| **Collateral (Guarantee Token)** | Medium-High (merchandise) | Liquidation value uncertainty |
| **Diversification (40+ deals)** | High (single-name limit 5%) | Correlated defaults |
| **Due Diligence** | Medium (8-12 week process) | Information asymmetry |
| **Covenants & Monitoring** | Medium (early warning) | Enforcement challenges |
| **OHADA Legal Enforcement** | Medium (harmonized law) | Varies by country |

**Historical Context:**

- African SME default rates: 8-12% (World Bank, 2025)
- Trade finance default rates: 3-5% (ICC, 2025)
- UJAMAA pilot (ZARA €2M): 0% default (1 deal, limited sample)

### 2.2 Concentration Risk

**Risk Description:**

Despite diversification, the pool may have concentrated exposure to:
- Single industrial (max 5%, but still material)
- Single sector (e.g., 40% in textiles)
- Single geography (e.g., 30% in Côte d'Ivoire)
- Single off-taker (e.g., ZARA accounts for 20% of repayments)

**Mitigation:**

- Single-name limit: 5% of NAV
- Sector limit: 40% of NAV
- Geographic limit: 30% of NAV
- Off-taker diversification requirements

### 2.3 Collateral Risk

**Risk Description:**

The Guarantee Token represents certified merchandise, but:
- Collateral value may decline (market price drops)
- Collateral may be damaged (fire, flood, theft)
- Collateral may be difficult to liquidate (specialized goods)
- Legal enforcement may be delayed (court backlog)

**Mitigation:**

- 100%+ collateral coverage required
- Insurance on warehouse stock
- GDIZ warehouse certification
- Regular stock audits

## 3. SMART CONTRACT & TECHNOLOGY RISK

### 3.1 Smart Contract Vulnerabilities

**Risk Description:**

Smart contracts may contain bugs or vulnerabilities that could result in:
- Loss of funds (exploit, drain)
- Incorrect calculations (NAV, yield)
- Frozen funds (cannot redeem)
- Unauthorized transfers (hacker access)

**Historical Precedents:**

| Incident | Date | Loss | Cause |
|----------|------|------|-------|
| **The DAO Hack** | 2016 | $60M | Reentrancy |
| **Parity Multisig** | 2017 | $150M | Access control |
| **Compound Oracle** | 2021 | $80M | Price manipulation |
| **Wormhole Bridge** | 2022 | $320M | Signature verification |
| **Total (2022-2025)** | - | $2B+ | Various |

**Mitigation Measures:**

| Mitigation | Status | Effectiveness |
|------------|--------|---------------|
| **CertiK Audit** | Q2 2026 | High (but not perfect) |
| **OpenZeppelin Audit** | Q2 2026 | High (but not perfect) |
| **Formal Verification** | Q2 2026 | High (critical functions) |
| **Bug Bounty ($100K-1M)** | Q2 2026 | Medium (incentivizes discovery) |
| **Timelock Upgrades (7-day)** | Implemented | High (community review) |
| **Emergency Pause** | Implemented | High (stop losses) |
| **Forta Monitoring** | Q2 2026 | Medium (anomaly detection) |
| **Insurance (Nexus)** | Phase 2 | Medium (coverage limits) |

**Residual Risk:** Despite mitigation, smart contract risk cannot be eliminated. Investors could lose 100% of funds in a catastrophic exploit.

### 3.2 Oracle Risk

**Risk Description:**

Chainlink oracles provide price feeds and external data, but:
- Oracle manipulation could affect NAV calculations
- Oracle downtime could delay distributions
- Flash loan attacks could manipulate prices

**Mitigation:**

- Chainlink decentralized oracle network
- Multiple price sources (aggregation)
- Time-weighted average price (TWAP)
- Circuit breakers on large price movements

### 3.3 Blockchain Risk

**Risk Description:**

Polygon/Ethereum network issues could affect:
- Transaction finality (network congestion)
- Gas costs (spikes during congestion)
- Network security (51% attack, though unlikely)
- Fork events (chain splits)

**Mitigation:**

- Polygon PoS (established network, 99.9% uLPime)
- Ethereum settlement layer (highest security)
- Gas price monitoring and optimization
- Multi-chain deployment (future)

### 3.4 Upgrade Risk

**Risk Description:**

Smart contract upgrades could:
- Introduce new bugs
- Change economic terms (if governance compromised)
- Cause temporary service disruLPion

**Mitigation:**

- 7-day timelock (community review)
- 3-of-5 multisig (no single point of failure)
- 5-of-5 for critical upgrades (unanimous)
- Public announcement of all upgrades

## 7. LIQUIDITY RISK

### 7.1 Lock-up Risk

**Risk Description:**

uLP Tokens have 180-365 day lock-up periods:
- Cannot redeem before lock-up expires
- Emergency access limited
- Opportunity cost (cannot redeploy)

**Mitigation:**

- Clear disclosure (lock-up terms)
- Staggered lock-ups (quarterly maturities)
- Secondary market (Phase 2 - BRVM listing)
- Liquidity reserve (5% of NAV)

### 7.2 Secondary Market Risk

**Risk Description:**

Even with secondary market:
- Limited buyers (accredited only)
- Wide bid-ask spreads
- Discount to NAV (illiquidity discount)
- ERC-3643 restrictions (verified buyers only)

**Mitigation:**

- Market makers (Phase 2)
- BRVM listing (regulated exchange)
- Investor education (long-term hold)

### 7.3 Redemption Gate Risk

**Risk Description:**

If >10% of NAV requested for redemption:
- Pro-rata distribution
- Delayed payments
- Potential fire sale of assets

**Mitigation:**

- Liquidity reserve (5%)
- Staggered maturities
- Gate provisions (fair treatment)

## 8. FX & CURRENCY RISK

### 8.1 Currency Mismatch Risk

**Risk Description:**

Although uLP is EURC-denominated:
- Some industrials earn in local currency (XOF, XAF, NGN)
- FX fluctuations affect repayment capacity
- EUR appreciation hurts exporters

**Historical Volatility:**

| Currency Pair | 1-Year Volatility | 5-Year Range |
|---------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **EUR/XOF** | 0% (pegged) | Fixed |
| **EUR/NGN** | 15% | 400-800 |
| **EUR/ZAR** | 12% | 15-22 |
| **EUR/USD** | 8% | 1.05-1.20 |

**Mitigation:**

- Export-oriented industrials (EUR revenues)
- Natural hedge (ZARA pays EUR)
- FX hedging instruments (Phase 2)
- XOF pegged to EUR (UEMOA countries)

### 8.2 Stablecoin Risk

**Risk Description:**

EURC is the denomination currency:
- De-pegging risk (EURC loses €1.00 peg)
- Issuer risk (Circle bankruLPcy)
- Reserve risk (insufficient backing)

**Mitigation:**

- EURC (regulated, audited reserves)
- Diversification (multiple stablecoins, Phase 2)
- Direct bank escrow (EUR fiat option)
