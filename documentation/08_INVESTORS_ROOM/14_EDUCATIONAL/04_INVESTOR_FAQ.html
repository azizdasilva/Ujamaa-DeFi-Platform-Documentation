1. GETTING STARTED

📄Q: What is UJAMAA DEFI PLATFORM?

A: UJAMAA DEFI PLATFORM is a regulated tokenization platform that enables fractional ownership of African real-world assets (real estate, agriculture, trade finance, renewable energy) through blockchain-based digital securities.

📄Q: Who can invest?

A: Eligibility depends on your jurisdiction and investor type:
- Retail Investors: Available in select African jurisdictions with minimum investments
- Accredited Investors: Available globally (subject to local laws)
- Institutional Investors: Welcome from most jurisdictions

See our Jurisdiction Eligibility Guide for details.

📄Q: How do I start investing?

A: Follow these steps:
1. Create an account on the Ujamaa DeFi Platform
2. Complete KYC verification (1-3 business days)
3. Verify accreditation (if required for certain offerings)
4. Set up a compatible crypto wallet
5. Fund your wallet with USDC/USDT
6. Browse offerings and subscribe

📄Q: What is the minimum investment?

A: Minimum investments vary by offering:
- Retail Offerings: Typically $100–$1,000
- Accredited Offerings: Typically $10,000–$50,000
- Institutional Offerings: Typically $100,000+

Check individual offering documents for specific minimums.

2. INVESTMENTS & OFFERINGS

📄Q: What types of assets are available?

A: We offer tokenized investments in:
- Real Estate: Commercial, residential, industrial properties
- Agriculture: Farms, cooperatives, plantations
- Trade Finance: Export receivables, invoice factoring
- Renewable Energy: Solar, wind, hydroelectric projects
- Infrastructure: Toll roads, ports, utilities
- Mining & Minerals: Royalty streams, production-sharing

📄Q: How are investments structured?

A: Each investment is held in a bankruLPcy-remote Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV). Tokens represent shares or beneficial ownership in the SPV, which owns the underlying asset.

📄Q: What due diligence is performed?

A: We conduct comprehensive due diligence:
- Legal: Title verification, regulatory compliance, contract review
- Financial: Audited financials, cash flow analysis, valuation
- Operational: Management assessment, operational history
- Technical: Independent engineering reports (where applicable)
- Environmental: Environmental impact assessments

📄Q: Are investments guaranteed?

A: No. Tokenized securities are not:
- Bank deposits
- FDIC/NCUA insured
- Government guaranteed
- Bank guaranteed

All investments carry risk, including potential loss of principal.

📄Q: How do I evaluate an offering?

A: Review these documents for each offering:
- Offering Memorandum: Full investment details
- Term Sheet: Key terms summary
- Risk Disclosure: All material risks
- Financial Projections: Expected performance
- Asset Documentation: Property reports, contracts, etc.

3. WALLET & TECHNOLOGY

📄Q: What wallet do I need?

A: You need a Web3 wallet that supports ERC-3643 tokens on Polygon:
- MetaMask (browser extension or mobile)
- Trust Wallet (mobile)
- Ledger/Trezor (hardware wallet + software)

See our Wallet Setup Guide for detailed instructions.

📄Q: Why Polygon and not Ethereum?

A: Polygon offers:
- Lower fees: ~$0.001–$0.01 vs. $5–$50 on Ethereum
- Faster transactions: ~2-5 minutes vs. 15+ minutes
- Ethereum compatibility: Same security model, lower cost
- African presence: Nodes in African data centers

📄Q: What are ERC-3643 tokens?

A: ERC-3643 is a token standard for regulated securities. Unlike regular tokens (ERC-20), ERC-3643:
- Requires identity verification to hold tokens
- Enforces transfer restrictions at the token level
- Supports compliance rules (holding periods, jurisdiction limits)

See our Understanding ERC-3643 Guide for details.

📄Q: What happens if I lose my private keys?

A: Unfortunately, lost private keys mean permanent loss of your tokens. There is no central authority that can recover lost keys. This is why secure key management is critical:
- Write down your seed phrase on paper
- Store it in multiple secure locations
- Never store it digitally
- Consider a hardware wallet for large holdings

📄Q: Can I use my exchange wallet?

A: Generally no. Exchange wallets (Binance, Coinbase, etc.) typically don't support ERC-3643 tokens or direct interaction with smart contracts. You need a self-custody wallet.

5. DISTRIBUTIONS & RETURNS

📄Q: How do I earn returns?

A: Returns come from:
- Distributions: Regular cash flow from asset operations (rent, sales, etc.)
- Appreciation: Increase in token value (NAV growth)
- Redemption: Selling tokens back at NAV (if permitted)

📄Q: How often are distributions paid?

A: Distribution frequency varies by offering:
- Real Estate: Typically quarterly
- Agriculture: Typically semi-annual (after harvest)
- Trade Finance: Typically monthly
- Renewable Energy: Typically quarterly

📄Q: How are distributions paid?

A: Distributions are paid automatically in USDC or USDT to your wallet. No action required on your part.

📄Q: What is the expected return?

A: Expected returns vary by asset class:

Asset Class Target IRR
Real Estate 8–12% annually
Agriculture 10–15% annually
Trade Finance 6–9% annually
Renewable Energy 8–12% annually
Infrastructure 7–10% annually

These are targets, not guarantees. Actual returns may vary.

📄Q: Are distributions guaranteed?

A: No. Distributions depend on asset performance. If an asset underperforms, distributions may be reduced or suspended.

6. TRANSFERS & LIQUIDITY

📄Q: Can I sell my tokens?

A: After any lock-up period, you may be able to:
- Transfer to another eligible investor
- Sell on a secondary market (if available)
- Redeem at NAV (if permitted by the offering)

📄Q: What is the lock-up period?

A: Lock-up periods vary by offering:
- Typical: 12–24 months
- Some offerings: No lock-up
- Some offerings: Longer lock-ups (3–5 years)

Check the specific offering terms.

📄Q: Is there a secondary market?

A: We are developing secondary market functionality. However:
- Liquidity is not guaranteed
- Secondary trading may be limited
- Prices may differ from NAV

📄Q: Can I transfer tokens to anyone?

A: No. ERC-3643 tokens can only be transferred to:
- Verified investors on the Ujamaa DeFi Platform
- Investors who meet eligibility requirements
- Investors in permitted jurisdictions

7. COMPLIANCE & KYC

📄Q: Why do I need to complete KYC?

A: KYC (Know Your Customer) verification is required by:
- Securities laws in all jurisdictions
- Anti-money laundering (AML) regulations
- FATF (Financial Action Task Force) requirements

📄Q: What documents do I need?

A: Typically required:
- Government ID: Passport, national ID, or driver's license
- Proof of Address: Utility bill or bank statement (< 3 months old)
- Selfie: For identity verification
- Accreditation Proof: For accredited offerings (financial statements, etc.)

See our KYC/AML Guide for details.

📄Q: How long does KYC take?

A: Processing times:
- Level 1 (Basic): Instant
- Level 2 (Enhanced): 1–3 business days
- Level 3 (Accredited): 3–5 business days

📄Q: What if my KYC expires?

A: KYC must be renewed periodically:
- Level 2: Every 24 months
- Level 3: Every 24 months (accreditation: 12 months)

You'll receive email reminders before expiry. Expired KYC may restrict new investments.

📄Q: Can I invest from any country?

A: No. Some jurisdictions are restricted:
- Comprehensive Sanctions: Iran, North Korea, Syria, Cuba, Crimea
- Other Restrictions: See our Jurisdiction Eligibility Guide

8. TAX & REPORTING

📄Q: What tax forms will I receive?

A: Depending on your jurisdiction:
- US Investors: Form 1099-DIV, potentially Schedule K-1
- Non-US Investors: Annual tax statement with distribution breakdown
- All Investors: Annual report with performance data

📄Q: How are distributions taxed?

A: Tax treatment varies by jurisdiction and distribution type:
- Ordinary Dividends: Taxed at ordinary income rates
- Capital Gains: Taxed at capital gains rates
- Return of Capital: Reduces cost basis (not immediately taxable)

Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

📄Q: Do I need to report foreign assets?

A: Possibly. Depending on your jurisdiction:
- US: FBAR (FinCEN 114) if foreign accounts >$10,000; Form 8938 for specified foreign assets
- EU: CRS reporting automatic
- Other: Check local requirements

📄Q: What records should I keep?

A: Keep records of:
- All subscription transactions
- All distribution receipts
- Annual tax statements
- Transaction history for token sales/transfers

9. SECURITY & SAFETY

📄Q: Is my investment secure?

A: We implement multiple security measures:
- Smart Contract Audits: Third-party audits before deployment
- Bug Bounty Program: Up to $250,000 for vulnerabilities
- Platform Security: Enterprise-grade security infrastructure
- Regulatory Compliance: Licensed and regulated entity

However, no investment is risk-free. See our Risk Disclosure Statement.

📄Q: Is UJAMAA DEFI PLATFORM regulated?

A: Yes. We hold licenses in multiple jurisdictions:
- Mauritius: FSC CIS Manager License
- Nigeria: SEC Nigeria Registered
- Kenya: CMA Licensed
- South Africa: FSCA FSP License

📄Q: What happens if UJAMAA DEFI PLATFORM shuts down?

A: Your tokens exist on the blockchain independently of the platform:
- Tokens remain in your wallet
- Underlying assets remain owned by the SPV
- Token holders retain their ownership rights
- A new administrator can be appointed if needed

📄Q: How do I avoid scams?

A: Follow these safety tips:
- Only use official website: app.Ujamaa DeFi Platform.com
- Never share your seed phrase or private keys
- Verify email senders (we'll never ask for sensitive info via email)
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Bookmark official URLs

10. TROUBLESHOOTING

📄Q: My transaction failed. What happened?

A: Common causes:
- Insufficient gas: Add more MATIC to your wallet
- Compliance check failed: Verify KYC status and transfer eligibility
- Network congestion: Wait and retry
- Slippage too low: Increase slippage tolerance

📄Q: I can't see my tokens in my wallet. What do I do?

A: Add the token manually:
1. Open your wallet
2. Click "Import Tokens" or "Add Token"
3. Enter the token contract address (from your investment documents)
4. Token should appear

📄Q: My KYC was rejected. What should I do?

A: You'll receive an email explaining the rejection. Common reasons:
- Expired ID: Renew and resubmit
- Blurry document: Retake photo in good lighting
- Address mismatch: Submit document with matching address

Resubmit with corrected documents.
