7. TAX FAQs

📄Q: How are distributions taxed?

A: Distribution taxation depends on your tax jurisdiction and the character of the distribution:
- Ordinary Dividends: Taxed at ordinary income rates
- Qualified Dividends: Taxed at preferential capital gains rates (US)
- Capital Gain Distributions: Taxed as capital gains
- Return of Capital: Reduces cost basis; not currently taxable

📄Q: Do I receive a Form K-1?

A: [Yes/No]. The Issuer is classified as a [CORPORATION/PARTNERSHIP] for tax purposes. [If partnership: You will receive a Schedule K-1 reporting your share of income, deductions, and credits.]

📄Q: How do I report token sales?

A: Token sales are generally treated as dispositions of property. Report gains/losses on:
- US: Form 8949 and Schedule D
- Other jurisdictions: Per local tax laws

📄Q: What is my cost basis?

A: Your cost basis is generally the amount you paid to acquire the tokens, including fees. See Section 5 for cost basis information.

📄Q: Do I need to file FBAR?

A: US persons with foreign financial accounts aggregating over $10,000 at any time during the year must file FinCEN Form 114.

📄Q: Is this investment subject to UBTI?

A: [Description of UBTI status]. Tax-exempt organizations should consult their tax advisor.

📄Q: What if I have a loss?

A: Capital losses may be deductible subject to limitations. Consult your tax advisor.

📄Q: How do I get an amended tax statement?

A: Contact tax@Ujamaa DeFi Platform.com if you believe your tax statement contains errors.
